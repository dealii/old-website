The deal.II Seminar Series

deal.II is a large project: There are many pieces you've probably never seen, and hundreds of applications you probably never thought about or didn't even think were possible to write with deal.II. It's also a a project in which a lot of people are involved, with ideas and careers worth talking about.

The deal.II Seminar Series is a way to talk about both scientific things, but also about the people involved. Seminars last about 45 minutes and are generally in more of a conversational style: Yes, the first 15-20 minutes are a scientific talk, followed by a few minutes of audience questions, but the rest is a conversation between the speaker and an experienced deal.II developer, possibly interspersed by interactive demonstrations, detours, and excursions — a fireside chat between computational scientists.

Seminars run live once a month on the last Friday of each month at 10:00 US East Coast Time (07:00 US Pacific Time; usually 16:00 Central European Time; usually 22:00 China Time). Recordings are also available at a later time via YouTube.
